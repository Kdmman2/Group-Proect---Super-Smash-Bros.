Super Smash Bros. 64

Super Smash Bros. or also known as Smash 64 was the first Super Smash Bros. game ever to come out. It was released on the Nintendo 64 in Japan on January 21, 1999, in North America on April 26th, 1999, and in Europe on November 19th, 1999. Super Smash Bros. is a crossover between several different Nintendo franchises, including Mario, Star Fox, Pokemon, Metroid, Zelda and more. Up to four playable characters can battle per match on the multiplayer mode. This game has a bunch of different stage locations, items and stage transformations from the different Nintendo games that make fighting on these stages unique and interesting. Super Smash Bros. on the Nintendo 64 marked the start of the Super Smash Bros. series which reached an unprecedented rise in popularity and gained all sorts of fans from around the world. The original game had 12 playable characters. The game starts the player out with eight chracters and the other four chracters are unlockable through various means. This game began a tradition of there being a Super Smash Bros. game that is released for every Nintendo console.
